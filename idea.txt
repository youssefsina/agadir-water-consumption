The Idea
We are building an AI-powered smart irrigation monitoring and leak detection
system for farmers.
The system uses sensor data (flow, pressure, soil moisture, and weather
information) to:
Detect early water leaks
Identify pipe bursts or blockages
Detect over-irrigation and under-irrigation
Automatically pause irrigation when rain is expected
Optimize irrigation based on soil moisture and temperature
The goal is to reduce water waste, lower energy costs, and improve crop health
using artificial intelligence.
The Problem
Agriculture consumes the majority of freshwater resources. However, many farms
still rely on manual irrigation or fixed schedules.
This leads to:
Undetected water leaks in irrigation pipelines
Over-irrigation that wastes water and damages crops
Under-irrigation that reduces yield
Irrigation running before rainfall
Increased electricity costs from unnecessary pump usage
Farmers usually detect these problems too late — after water loss or crop stress
has already occurred.

Devices Needed
1. Flow Meter
Installed after the irrigation pump on the main pipeline.
Measures water flow (liters per minute).
2. Pressure Sensor
Installed on the irrigation pipe.
Detects pressure drops or abnormal pressure patterns.
3. Soil Moisture Sensor
Installed in the soil near crops.
Measures how wet or dry the soil is.
4. Temperature Sensor
Measures ambient temperature.
Used to estimate evaporation and irrigation needs.
5. Weather Data

Decision Engine (Rules Layer)
On top of AI, we add agricultural rules:
Weather Rule:
If rain probability > 60% and expected rainfall > 2mm
→ Pause irrigation
Soil Rule:
If soil moisture > 60%
→ Stop irrigation
If soil moisture < 25% and no rain predicted
→ Allow irrigation
Over-Irrigation Rule:
If irrigation duration exceeds historical baseline
→ Alert
Under-Irrigation Rule:
If scheduled ON but flow ≈ 0
→ Pump or valve issue alert

Pitch Script
Water scarcity is one of the biggest challenges in agriculture. Farmers lose water
and money due to undetected irrigation leaks, over-irrigation, and poor adaptation
to weather conditions.
We built an AI-powered smart irrigation monitoring system that learns normal
irrigation behavior and detects abnormal patterns in real time.
Using simulated sensor data from flow meters, pressure sensors, soil moisture
sensors, and weather forecasts, our system can:
Detect early pipeline leaks
Identify over and under irrigation
Pause irrigation when rain is predicted
Optimize watering based on soil conditions
The system combines machine learning using Isolation Forest with intelligent
agricultural decision rules.
